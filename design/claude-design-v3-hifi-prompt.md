# Claude Design prompt — Little Fables hi-fi prototype (The Drawn Room)

Paste below the line into the **same Claude Design project that contains the approved Drawn Room design system** — the prototype must consume its tokens (`--paper`, `--ink`, the 10 pigments, `data-register`), the RoomScene kit, and every component (Buddy, ReadingPage, AskCard, ChoiceCards, BreatheCircle, ReadingSuns, StarWord, Medallion, RecapStrip). No new visual language: if a screen needs something the system lacks, extend the system's logic, don't invent a second one.

---

Build the complete interactive hi-fi prototype of **Little Fables** as one click-through app (iPad landscape 1180×820 primary; portrait stacks of Home and Reader only). Real content throughout: the shelf carries Miko and the Wobbly Bridge (3 chapters, real ink-and-wash art), The Moose Who Knew About Bigness, Papa Gets the Moon, The Cozy Circle, Bramble's Hello, Azi's Little Bhen. Real vocab (wobbly, steady, grateful, bhen), real family words (bhen, agua, chalo). Simulations stand in for live systems (word highlight on a timer; mic resolves after ~1.5s; generation waits ~4s) — note each with a comment naming the real system it stands for.

## The flows to build (all connected)

**1. First arrival.** Endpaper open beat (wash field + tiny motif — this is also the app's loading state, there is no spinner anywhere) → the room, morning light, no buddy yet → meeting the buddies: they're IN the room (peeking from behind the shelf, on the windowsill), not in a carousel; tapping/greeting one hears its intro; choosing it = it walks to the rug. Include the bear, otter, little anky, moto, rocky, rusty; one wrapped crate under the table ("someone new is coming — read 2 more days!").

**2. Home — the room, living.** The approved north-star room as the working Home: buddy breathing on the rug speaking exactly ONE line (greeting, or world-memory callback like "The web bridge you built is still standing!" only when history exists — show both variants); **one terracotta beacon** on the next-best action (the mid-flight book glowing on the shelf, or today's pick); reading suns on the windowsill (lit suns never unlight); star words pinned on the wall; badge medallions on the shelf edge; his own authored books with the distinct drawn spine; window showing real time of day. States: fresh-start morning, mid-flight afternoon (Continue beacon), evening (lantern register begins at the window). Offline state: a drawn paper note on the door ("No internet — your saved stories still work"). ≤5 child decision points, everything speaks on press-and-hold.

**3. Voice agency (v3 R16–R18).** The buddy is the mic: tapped → leans in, breathes, listens (no red dots) → on intent, the target object in the room glows terracotta + buddy line ("The otter book? Here we GO!") → navigation. Show: successful intent, low-confidence misfire (buddy offers two concrete spoken options drawn from the room), and the two-miss fallback (buddy points, tap path highlighted). Voice can never reach parent surfaces.

**4. Opening a book → Reader.** Book lifts from shelf → room edges fall away → endpaper beat → `data-register="story"`: the page IS the screen, art + text on paper, chrome reduced to ink (page count as a drawn folio corner, quiet back arrow). Word highlight = lamplight glow crossing words. Build these page states with real Miko content: plain reading page; **ask** (AskCard + mic ≥56px, listening, praise in the buddy's voice using the child's actual words, gentle hint after two misses); **choice** (ChoiceCards as drawn objects — Boulder's neck vs. Miko's moto — plus the spoken freeform third path "or tell me YOUR idea!"; chosen card glows, others recede; then "your choice is changing the story…" as ink writing itself across the page bottom, NOT dots); **breathe-along** (BreatheCircle swelling like a wash bloom on Miko's belly-breath page).

**5. Chapter end + resume.** Chapter end: buddy cheer, one spoken recap question with mic, hook line ("Next time: Crossing Day…"), giant terracotta **Next chapter** + quiet **All done for now** (returning = the room again, book returning to shelf with its bookmark ribbon advanced). Resume after absence: RecapStrip — "Last time…" as three drawn panels with the child's own choice highlighted ("YOU chose Boulder's long neck!") → terracotta ▶.

**6. Book complete → Tell it back → badge.** Book-complete celebration in lantern light (light does the celebrating — glow blooms, dust motes; no confetti-noise): vocab StarWords to tap (each speaks word + meaning, then flies to the wall pins), Tell-it-back with the drawn record button (breathing idle, wash-bloom while recording with soft timer, "Saved for Mama and Papa" as a drawn envelope going onto the shelf). If a badge triggered: Medallion earn moment — lantern-lit, buddy speaks the earned line, medallion settles onto the shelf edge.

**7. The story kitchen (v3 R19–R23) — the flagship flow, most care here.** Entry: child says "make a story" / the little writing desk in the room. The idea interview at the desk: buddy asks 2–3 questions (want → why → what-could-go-wrong), each answer acknowledged in the buddy's next line using the child's words; silence is fine (buddy fills in). Read-back: buddy repeats the whole recipe as a story-opening and asks "Did I get it right?". **The writing moment (R22a):** a little open book on the desk, the child's transcribed words appearing onto its pages in watercolor handwriting while the buddy narrates softly — this doubles as the generation wait; reduced-motion variant fades words in. Then: the new book appears ON the shelf with "by Azad" and his drawn spine mark, first-creation **Storyteller** medallion moment. Also mock the out-of-bounds redirect ("Zombies aren't in our world… but Peter is the sneakiest trickster there IS — should Peter be the spooky one?") and the daily-cap line, in-fiction, no sad buddy ("the story desk needs to rest until tomorrow — let's READ one instead!").

**8. My Words wall + quiet mode.** Tapping the pinned words on the wall zooms to the Language Wall: words grouped by language (Gujarati / Spanish / Hindi / Creole), each card speaks word + meaning + which story it came from; Mystery-Word found moment (a word flying to the wall with a small light bloom). Quiet mode: the room at night — lantern register, moon in the window, the wind-down books (Cozy Circle, Papa Gets the Moon) softly lit; everything slower and lower.

**9. Grown-ups (system boundary).** Gate (3-answer multiplication, shake) and Parent Corner exactly per the system's parent-surface rule: shadcn neutrals, Inter, no drawn elements — Stories (with QA status pills), Retellings (player + transcript), **Made by Azad** (interview transcript + recipe + archive/keep), Profile & creative guardrails (themes, cast + wildcard slots, cap, excludeTerms). One screen each is enough; density over polish here.

## Rules (bind everything)

Exactly one terracotta action per screen — when a mic or choice is active, other beacons recede to ink. Every interactive element ≥56px and speaks; the app is fully operable without reading. No spinners (endpapers), no cuts within scenes (objects and camera move continuously), no grey shadows, no loss states, no sad-buddy reactions to any child choice, no hand-drawn UI text (only the child's own words in the writing moment). Reduced-motion variants noted per animation. Buddy speech ≤1 line per screen visit.

## Acceptance tests (run against the finished prototype, state results)

The 9am test: cold open → page one of a chosen book in ≤2 interactions. The squint test on every kid screen: one obvious glowing next thing. The silly test: every screen framed-not-cringed. The car test: Reader operable start-to-finish by voice + the single terracotta control. The interview test: the kitchen flow's generated read-back demonstrably contains the child's want, reason, and obstacle.
