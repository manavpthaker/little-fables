# Claude Design prompt — Little Fables (complete app, from scratch)

Paste everything below the line into Claude Design (claude.ai/design), in the project that contains the **Little Fables Design System**. Design fresh from this brief — the design system is the only inherited artifact.

---

Design the complete UI for **Little Fables**: an interactive reading app for one 4-year-old, Azad (reads and reasons at a 5–6 level), on an **iPad in landscape (1180×820)**. Parents write the books; Azad reads, listens, answers out loud, makes choices, and retells stories in his own words. It's a daytime and car-ride app — weekend mornings and drives, never bedtime.

Use the Little Fables Design System exactly: Dream Paper palette (cream #FBF4E6 canvas, espresso ink, **coral #F4813C as the ONE action color — exactly once per screen**, pastels label but never act), Baloo 2 display + Quicksand body, pill/squircle/arch shape language, warm shadows only, ink-and-wash watercolor as the illustration style. Parent-facing surfaces use the system's shadcn neutrals with Inter — quiet, plain, no emoji, no exclamation points.

## The world

- **Stories:** "Miko and the Wobbly Bridge" is a 3-chapter chapter book (a fox on a blue moto, a wobbly bridge over Dino Canyon, a spider's web, a gentle brontosaurus — art exists in this style). "Azi's Little Bhen" and "Jujy's Christmas Adventure" are one-shot quick stories. Real vocab words: wobbly, steady, grateful, bhen (Gujarati for little sister).
- **The Buddy:** Azad's guide through the app, chosen from a carousel of personified characters you'll design in the watercolor style: **Bear** (cozy, brave), **Otter** (playful water-lover), **Little Ankylosaurus** (shy, sturdy) — living; **Moto** (a motorcycle, vroom jokes), **Rocky** (a rock, deadpan), **Rusty** (a toy rocket, dreams of space) — nonliving. Each buddy's spoken intro owns its nature: "I'm Rocky. I'm a rock. I don't eat or grow — but I'm a GREAT listener." Living/nonliving is a running teaching thread, shown as a tiny leaf tag vs. pebble tag. The chosen buddy appears on Home and key moments: it greets Azad by name, remembers his story choices aloud ("The web bridge you built is still standing!"), points to what's next, and celebrates wins.
- **Progress that never punishes:** reading days are suns on a weekly row — a day with reading lights its sun; suns never turn off, gaps are simply unlit and neutral. Badges are watercolor medallions (Miko Master, 5 Reading Days, First Choice, 10 Star Words) that recognize but never gate. Star words collect into a word book. No coins, gems, XP, timers, locks, leaderboards, or notifications — ever.

## Screens (linked click-through)

1. **Buddy carousel** (first launch; reachable from Home): big swipeable buddy cards, one centered and "speaking" its intro in a speech bubble; leaf/pebble tag; coral "Pick me!" on the centered card. At the carousel's end, one **arrival crate**: a wiggling wooden crate — "A new buddy is coming — read 2 more days!" (a coming attraction, not a lock).
2. **Buddy arrival** — three beats: crate waiting, crate cracking open mid-chapter-progress, reveal celebration with the new buddy stepping out.
3. **Home** — four zones: (a) **buddy header**: the buddy + exactly one speech-bubble line (greeting or memory callback) + seven small suns for this week; (b) **Continue** (dominant when a book is mid-flight): cover art, mini chapter dots (● ● ▶ ○), progress ring, coral ▶ — otherwise **Today's adventure**: featured story + one-line daily quest ("Read one chapter and find a new star word"); (c) **My World strip**: latest 3 badges + count, star-word count — both tappable; (d) **Bookshelf**: two rows — Chapter books (covers with progress rings) and Quick stories — every cover in a cream mat so mixed art reads as one family, plus the line "New stories appear here when Mom and Dad finish making them ✦". Nav: a floating pill with exactly Home / Grown-ups.
4. **Chapter map** (opening a chapter book): chapters as illustrated stops on a short winding path; finished stops show scene-art thumbnails with ✓, the current stop is largest with a coral ▶ and the buddy standing beside it, future stops are soft closed-book silhouettes ("not yet" — never a padlock). Finished stops re-open for re-reading.
5. **Reader** — picture-book spread: full art on the left page (support full-bleed variants), words on the right in a cream reading card at ≥24px with word-by-word highlight (peach fill + coral underline tracking the narration). Below the words, teaching moments appear in sequence: an **ask** (question + pulsing coral mic + listening state + praise state in mint / gentle hint state in amber), a **breathe-along** ask variant (for emotional-regulation moments: a large soft circle that slowly grows and shrinks with "In… and out…" — the child breathes with the character; no mic, completes on its own), or a **choice** (2–3 big emoji-and-label cards, "Say it out loud — or tap it!", chosen card glows coral while others dim, then a "Your choice is changing the story…" moment with bouncing dots). Chapter progress bar with a knob; controls: quiet prev/replay circles flanking one large coral Next that visually recedes (cream, muted) whenever a mic or choice is the active action.
6. **Chapter end** — buddy cheers, one spoken recap question with mic, the next-chapter hook ("Next time: the cave door creaks open…"), and two giant actions: coral **Next chapter**, quiet cream **All done for now**.
7. **Book complete** — bigger celebration: vocab stars to tap (each speaks its word + meaning), the **Tell it back** card (retell prompts + one large coral record button with breathing idle, pulsing-ring recording with a timer, and a mint "Saved for Mom and Dad!" state), and a badge handoff when one is earned.
8. **Badge earning** — full-screen: watercolor medallion scales in with confetti, buddy speaks the earned line ("You finished all 3 Miko books!"), single coral continue. Plus the **badge shelf**: earned badges bright, next-up badges as silhouettes with a one-line how-to-earn.
9. **My Words** — the word book: a warm grid of word cards (word + tiny chip of the story cover it came from); a tapped card expands to the meaning with a speaker glyph ("wobbly — shaky and wibbly, not steady").
10. **Recap on resume** — brief interstitial: buddy + "Last time…" two-line summary + his previous choice highlighted ("YOU chose Boulder's long neck!") + coral ▶.
11. **Grown-ups** — parent surfaces in shadcn neutrals/Inter: (a) a gate — one multiplication question, three tappable answers, gentle shake + "Not quite — try once more." on a miss; (b) **Parent corner** — stories the family has made (covers, author, pages, status pills: Draft → Checking → Published, with "art still painting" for drafts, a "New story" primary button), Azad's retell recordings (play rows with progress), and the universe editor (interest chips, teaching-goal toggles, Gujarati family words as word/meaning rows), with a quiet "Auto-saved" footer; (c) **Story maker** (parent-facing, same neutral system) — a guided wizard: step 1 pick format (Quick story / Chapter book 3–5 chapters), step 2 a short conversational intake (one question per card: who is the hero, what do they want, where does it happen, what should it teach — with suggestion chips drawn from the child's interests and teaching goals), step 3 review + "Write it" with a generating state and a per-chapter progress list.
12. **Edge states** — offline banner on Home ("No internet — your saved stories still work!") and the kid-facing generation error ("The story machine hiccuped. Try again!").

## Rules

Every interactive element pairs a visual cue with spoken audio (show speech bubbles or speaker glyphs wherever audio happens) — a child who cannot read must be able to operate every kid flow. Primary kid touch targets ≥56px, everything ≥44px. Kid copy: short rhythmic sentences, sound effects in CAPS!, praise echoes the child's answer in CAPS, hints are phonetic and kind, never "wrong." No loss states, no dark patterns, rewards stay inside the fiction. Reduced motion respected. Landscape 1180×820 is primary; also mock portrait stacks of Home and the chapter map only.
