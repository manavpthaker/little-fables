## Quick-start setup

### Prerequisites
- Read the complete Azi-Verse Universe Guide for character personalities and world details
- Review the Future-Ready Skills Framework for age-appropriate skill targets
- Understand the Parent-Child Story Development research for interactive storytelling techniques
- Familiarize yourself with the Evaluation Rubric scoring criteria

### Story creation workflow
1. **Target selection:** Choose age band (0-3, 4-8, 7-10) and specific future-ready skills to embed
2. **Universe grounding:** Select Azi-Verse setting, characters, and cultural elements from universe guide
3. **Structure planning:** Apply multi-layer narrative template (entertainment + skill building + values)
4. **Draft creation:** Write using established tone, language patterns, and character voices
5. **Self-evaluation:** Score against rubric criteria before submission
6. **Cultural verification:** Confirm authentic representation of Colombian/Indian elements
7. **Iteration:** Refine based on rubric feedback until publication-ready (90+ score)

## Coding conventions

### Story structure by age band

- 0-100 words total, 8-24 pages
- Simple 3-beat pattern (problem-attempt-solution)
- 1 heritage word maximum with visual context
- Sensory elements and repetitive patterns
- End with comfort (snack, song, or moon-gazing)

- ~500 words across 32 pages
- Clear problem-solution arc with gentle adventure
- 1-2 heritage words per page with context clues
- "Prediction pause" opportunities built in
- Interactive elements for parent-child engagement

- 4,000-10,000 words in 10 chapters
- Complex multi-layer integration
- Natural multilingual dialogue flow
- Sophisticated ethical dilemmas
- Real-world application opportunities

### Character voice standards
- **Azi:** Thoughtful, stubborn, imaginative; processes through music and puzzles
- **Jujy:** Confident leader who means well but sometimes forgets plans
- **Dory:** Spacey and cheerful with zero memory for details
- **Pandies:** Wise, slow-speaking guide who says just enough
- **Clappy:** Joyful musician who feels things out loud

### Language integration rules
- Code-switching must feel natural, never forced
- Heritage words require context clues: "Qu quieres?" Mama asked. "What do you want for lunch?"
- Maximum 1-2 heritage words per page to avoid overwhelm
- Pronunciation guides in backmatter for family use

### Cultural authenticity requirements
- Verify accuracy with Colombian/Indian community members
- Avoid mixing unrelated cultural traditions
- Focus on authentic family experiences, not stereotypes
- Include both cultures equally and respectfully

## Deployment & ci/cd

### Story evaluation pipeline
1. **Self-scoring:** Complete rubric evaluation before submission
2. **Peer review:** Colleague assessment for narrative quality
3. **Cultural review:** Heritage language accuracy and cultural authenticity
4. **Developmental review:** Age-appropriateness confirmation
5. **Final scoring:** Must achieve 90+ for publication readiness

### Quality gates by criteria
- **Age Appropriateness:** Content matches target developmental stage
- **Story Structure:** Multi-layer narrative with clear arc
- **Cultural Authenticity:** Respectful, accurate representation verified by community
- **Language Elements:** Natural multilingual integration
- **Future-Ready Skills:** Seamlessly embedded without preaching
- **Universe Consistency:** Characters and world align with established guide

### Revision cycle
- Score below 90: Return to draft with specific improvement notes
- Score 90+: Proceed to final cultural verification
- Cultural concerns: Address with community consultant input
- Ready for publication: Add to Azi-Verse story collection

## Troubleshooting & faqs

### Common story creation issues

A: Use maximum 1-2 words per page. Provide immediate context. Test with multilingual families. Example: "Agua!" Azi called out. "Water, please!"

A: Embed through character actions, not explanation. Show Azi asking better questions (critical thinking) or helping friends resolve conflicts (emotional intelligence).

A: Reference specific character descriptions. Jujy is confident but forgetful. Dory is spacey but loving. Maintain core traits while allowing story-appropriate responses.

A: Surface layer = engaging adventure. Skills layer = problem-solving. Values layer = ethical choices. Systems layer = understanding consequences. Future layer = exploring outcomes.

A: Focus on specific family traditions, not broad generalizations. Show Azi's actual experiences: video calls with Lito/Lita, Dada's Hess trucks, Mama's Colombian cooking.

### Skill integration examples
- **AI Literacy:** Azi learns to ask better questions of his educational apps
- **Emotional Intelligence:** Characters name feelings and find healthy coping strategies
- **Cultural Intelligence:** Friends navigate differences with curiosity and respect
- **Systems Thinking:** Actions in garden affect whole ecosystem; family decisions impact community

### Age-appropriate complexity
- **Ages 0-3:** Basic emotional naming, simple cause-effect
- **Ages 4-8:** Problem-solving choices, cultural curiosity, friendship skills
- **Ages 7-10:** Ethical reasoning, strategic thinking, collaborative planning

## Coding conventions

### Recurring Azi-Verse elements to include
- **Opening ritual:** Often starts in playroom or with "whisker-wiggle roll call"
- **Cultural moments:** Natural code-switching, family traditions
- **Problem-solving:** Characters work together using different strengths
- **Comfort ending:** Snack (arepas, samosas, honey), song, or moon-watching
- **Growth moment:** Quiet realization or skill development

### Tone and voice guidelines
- Gentle, elevated, timeless narrator who observes kindly
- Lyrical without being lofty
- Authentic multilingual family speech patterns
- Humor that's quiet and kind
- Understanding that some jokes translate, some don't, but love always does

### Interactive elements to embed
- Natural pause points for "What do you think happens next?"
- Questions that invite prediction and discussion
- Cultural learning moments for whole family
- Problem-solving opportunities where readers can suggest solutions
