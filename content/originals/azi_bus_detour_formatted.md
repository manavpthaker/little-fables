# Azi and the Day the Yellow Bus Took a Detour

*A story about finding your way when plans change*

It was Monday morning in the playroom at The Westfield, and Azi sat cross-legged on the circular rug with his guitar across his lap. The autumn light spilled through the window where Papa's Gujarati tablet rested quietly, catching the colorful spines of books tucked everywhere.

"¡Whisker-wiggle roll call!" Azi announced, strumming his three-chord progression: C-G-Am.

"Jujy here!" called the black-and-white tuxedo cat, straightening his invisible collar. "Ready to lead today's expedition to Wardlaw Hartridge!"

"Dory here!" sang the gray-and-white cat, already forgetting she'd just said her name.

"Pandies, present and accounted for," rumbled the wise panda from his driver's position near the door.

"Citie, observing," whispered the quiet companion.

Clappy bounded over, red and round and full of rhythm. "Clappy ready to clap for whatever happens!"

Loki folded a perfect origami crane while announcing, "Loki here, thinking ahead."

And Inu added softly, "Inu present, counting to ten in Hindi: ek, do, teen..."

Azi grinned and played the closing chord. "¡Todos listos!" he called out. "Everyone ready for camp!"

But when they climbed aboard Pandies' yellow bus, something felt different.

"Hmm," Pandies said slowly, checking his invisible ranger hat. "Seems the usual route is blocked today. Construction on Elm Street."

Azi's stomach did a little flip. He liked knowing exactly which streets they'd take, which traffic light would be red, which house had the friendly dog in the yard.

"No problem!" Jujy announced confidently. "We'll just... um..." He paused, forgetting his plan halfway through.

"Take the mystery route?" suggested Dory cheerfully.

"There's no such thing as a mystery route," Azi said, his voice getting smaller. He strummed his guitar nervously: C-G-Am, C-G-Am.

Clappy clapped once, then stopped when he realized everyone was worried.

"What if we get lost?" Azi asked. "What if we're late? What if—"

"What if," Loki said gently, unfolding his crane, "we think about this like a puzzle?"

Citie, who noticed everything from her quiet spot, pointed out the window. "That street connects to Warren Avenue."

"And Warren leads to the community center," added Inu. "I remember from when we went there for the Diwali celebration."

"¡La celebración!" Azi's eyes lit up. "I remember too! Dadi brought those amazing samosas."

Pandies nodded slowly. "Community center route it is. Everyone hold on."

As the bus turned down an unfamiliar street, Azi felt his chest get tight. This wasn't the plan. Plans were supposed to work.

But then Loki handed him the origami crane. "Sometimes the best path isn't the one you planned."

And Dory added, "Look! New things to see!"

Outside the window, there was a garden Azi had never noticed, full of late-blooming marigolds that reminded him of the ones Mama planted every spring. A little girl was watering them with her grandmother, both of them chatting in what sounded like Portuguese.

"Different families, same love," Inu observed quietly.

"You know what?" Azi said, his three-chord pattern shifting to something gentler. "This reminds me of when Lito calls from Colombia and the internet gets fuzzy. We have to find different ways to connect."

"Like when your Mama sings in español and I don't know the words, but I can still clap along," Clappy added, bouncing a little.

"Or when Papa reads from his Gujarati tablet and the letters look like music to me," Azi continued, feeling the knot in his chest loosening.

Jujy puffed up proudly. "See? I knew this detour would work out!"

"You were scared too," Dory pointed out with a giggle.

"Was not!" Jujy protested, then grinned. "Okay, maybe a little."

Pandies chuckled from the driver's seat. "Sometimes the best leaders admit when they're figuring it out too."

Twenty minutes later, they pulled into the familiar Wardlaw Hartridge parking lot.

"We made it," Azi said, surprised at how calm he felt.

"Different path, same destination," Loki noted, refolding his crane into something new.

"And we learned something," Citie added. "There are lots of ways to get where you need to go."

As they gathered their backpacks, Azi played one more chord progression on his guitar—but this time, he added a little flourish at the end.

"¿Qué fue eso?" asked a familiar voice. Mama had come to walk him to the camp entrance.

"What was that extra note, mi amor?"

"That," Azi said, slinging his guitar over his shoulder, "was the sound of finding a new way home."

That afternoon, back in the playroom, Azi pulled out his felt-tip markers and drew a map on construction paper. He drew their usual route in blue, and the new community center route in green.

"Two ways to get there," he told his companions during afternoon snack time, passing around honey crackers Papa had picked up from Pooh's Honey Farm.

"Maybe tomorrow we'll discover a third," suggested Dory.

"Maybe," Azi agreed, taking a bite of honey sweetness. "And that's okay too."

Outside his bedroom window that night, the moon watched over all the children who had learned something new that day—about flexibility, about trust, about the many paths that can lead you exactly where you need to be.

And in the gentle darkness, Azi hummed his three chords one more time, adding that extra flourish that sounded like discovery, like courage, like coming home by a different route.

*C-G-Am... and one note more.*

**The End**
