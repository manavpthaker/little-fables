# The Cozy Circle

*An Azi-Verse Bedtime Story*

The day had been big.

Big playing. Big feelings. Big everything.

Now Azi's room was getting dark, and the space between his bed and the door felt very, very far.

"Stay," Azi whispered to Mama.

"I'll be right outside," Mama said. "And you won't be alone."

Jujy stood up a little straighter on the pillow.

"Night Watch time," he announced quietly. "I've got this."

Dory yawned. "Got what?"

"The... the thing." Jujy paused. "The important thing."

"Oh good," said Dory, already forgetting what they were talking about.

Slothie didn't say anything at all.

Slothie just breathed.

In... slow... out... slower.

Baby Slothie tucked against Slothie's arm, rising and falling like a tiny boat on a gentle wave.

Azi's body still felt too awake. Too buzzy. Too much.

"My tummy feels wiggly," he said.

Pandies nodded slowly from the foot of the bed. "Wiggly feelings like to visit at bedtime. They're looking for a place to rest."

"Where do they rest?"

"In the Cozy Circle."

Pandies spoke in his slow, steady voice:

"First, your blanket goes here—" He tucked it around Azi's feet.

"Then your friends go here—" Jujy, Dory, Slothie, all in their spots.

"Then your breathing goes like this—" Slow in. Slower out.

"And that makes a circle. A Cozy Circle. Nothing gets in that doesn't belong. Nothing gets out that wants to stay."

Azi looked at his circle.

Blanket. Friends. Breath.

The wiggly feeling in his tummy didn't disappear.

But it got quieter.

Like it had found somewhere to sit down.

"What if I need Mama?" Azi whispered.

Jujy puffed up. "I'll get her! I'll—wait, can I open doors?"

"No," said Dory dreamily.

"Right. Well. I'll... think very hard about opening the door."

Pandies spoke again, gently:

"Mama is right outside. That's very close. And she always comes back to check. That's her promise."

"Like the moon's promise?"

"Just like."

Azi looked at the window.

The moon was there.

It didn't say anything, because moons don't talk.

But it watched. It had always watched. It would keep watching until morning came.

"Slothie," Azi whispered. "Can we count?"

Slothie breathed in.

"One star," Azi said.

Slothie breathed out.

"Two stars."

In.

"Three stars."

Out.

Somewhere around star number seven, the wiggly feeling settled all the way down.

Somewhere around star number nine, Azi's eyes got heavy.

Somewhere around star number ten—

The Cozy Circle held.

Jujy kept watch (mostly).

Dory dreamed beside him.

Slothie barely moved.

And the moon glowed soft and steady, watching over all the children who were learning that the distance between bed and door isn't so far after all.

Mama peeked in, just like she promised.

Azi was already dreaming.

But somewhere in his dream, he knew she came.

And that was enough.

**The End**

**Target Age:** 3-5 years  
**Reading Time:** 5 minutes  
**Themes:** Separation anxiety, emotional regulation, bedtime resistance  
**Key Tools:** Cozy Circle (externalized boundary), Slothie's Star Counting (breathing), Jujy's Night Watch (companions stay), Moon's Promise (parent check-ins)

## Implementation Guide

### Real-Life Invocations

Use these phrases at actual bedtime to connect story to reality:

| Situation | What to Say |
|-----------|-------------|
| Starting bedtime routine | "Let's make your Cozy Circle. Blanket, friends, breathing." |
| When he says tummy hurts/feels weird | "The wiggly feelings are looking for a place to rest." |
| When he asks you to stay | "I'll check on you—that's my promise." |
| To begin calming | "Let's do Slothie's counting. One star... two stars..." |
| If he gets out of bed | "Back to the Cozy Circle. It's waiting for you." |

### Why This Story Works for Azi's Challenges

**Addresses Separation Anxiety:**
- Validates that the distance "feels very far" (normalizes the feeling)
- Companions stay when parents leave (transitional objects)
- Parent always comes back to check (predictable promise)
- The check happens even when he's asleep (he still *knows*)

**Addresses Emotional Dysregulation:**
- Acknowledges big feelings without trying to fix them
- The wiggly feeling doesn't disappear—it finds a place to rest
- Breathing ritual embedded naturally (Slothie's counting)
- Physical/concrete tool (the Cozy Circle) externalizes the boundary

**Addresses Bedtime Resistance:**
- Shows successful falling asleep (modeling the outcome)
- Doesn't negotiate or explain—just holds the frame
- Characters are calm and steady (borrowed regulation)
- Short, repetitive, calming rhythm

### Reading Tips

1. **Read slowly** — especially Pandies' parts. You're modeling calm.

2. **Breathe along** — When counting stars, actually breathe with the rhythm.

3. **Keep voice soft and steady** — You are the regulation he borrows.

4. **Use the same words each time** — Predictability is the point.

5. **If he asks you to stay** — Gently repeat: "I'll check on you. That's my promise." Don't negotiate.

6. **After the story** — Actually do the Cozy Circle setup: blanket, position friends, do 3-5 star breaths together.

### Developmental Alignment

**Age:** 3 years 8 months

**Core need:** External regulation before internal regulation is possible
